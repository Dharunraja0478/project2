Tenses

Tenses in grammar refer to the time at which an action takes place or the state of being. They help convey when something happens â€“ whether in the past, present, or future. There are various tenses, each serving a specific purpose in expressing actions or states in relation to time.

Tense Categories:

Present Tense:

Simple Present: Describes habitual actions, general truths, or regular occurrences.

Formula : Subject + Base Verb (s/es for third person singular)

Example: I play tennis every Sunday.

Present Continuous: Indicates an action in progress at the current moment.

Formula : Formula: Subject + am/is/are + Present Participle

Example: She is reading a book.


Past Tense:

Simple Past: Represents completed actions in the past.

Formula : Subject + Past Verb

Example: They went to the store yesterday.

Past Continuous: Describes actions that were ongoing in the past.

Formula : Subject + was/were + Present Participle

Example: We were studying when the phone rang.


Future Tense:

Simple Future: Expresses actions that will occur in the future.

Formula : Subject + will/shall + Base Verb

Example: I will finish the project by next week.

Future Continuous:Depicts actions that will be ongoing at a specific time in the future.

Formula : Subject + will/shall + be + Present Participle

Example: They will be celebrating their anniversary tomorrow.


Perfect Tenses:

Present Perfect: Indicates actions completed at an unspecified time before the present.

Formula : Subject + have/has + Past Participle

Example: She has visited London.

Past Perfect: Shows an action completed before another action in the past.

Formula : Subject + had + Past Participle

Example: By the time we arrived, they had already left.

Future Perfect:Forecasts an action that will be completed before a specific time in the future.

Formula : Subject + will/shall + have + Past Participle

Example: They will have finished the assignment by Friday.


Perfect Continuous Tenses:

Present Perfect Continuous: Describes continuous actions that started in the past and continue into the present.

Formula :Subject + have/has + been + Present Participle

Example: I have been working on this project for weeks.

Past Perfect Continuous: Depicts continuous actions that were ongoing before another action in the past.

Formula : Subject + had + been + Present Participle

Example: She had been waiting for hours when the bus finally arrived.

Future Perfect Continuous: Foretells continuous actions that will be ongoing before a specific point in the future.

Formula : Subject + will/shall + have + been + Present Participle

Example: By this time next year, they will have been living here for a decade.
